The STEM Cycle of Inquiry: Ask Stage

For this investigation, you will be exploring the following question:

How biodiverse is my local waterway?

Copy the question you are investigating on page 3 of your logbook

02/06

The STEM Cycle of Inquiry: Predict Stage

A prediction is what you think will happen in an experiment, before you do it.

Use the following template to write your guess.

"If I look at microorganisms under a microscope, then I think I will see ____ because ____ "

If I...What will you change or test?
Then I think...What do you think will happen?
Because...Why do you think that? Use your own ideas or what you already know.

Example: If I look at microorganisms under a microscope, then I think I will see lots of different organisms because I think the waterway will have lots of biodiversity.

Write your prediction on page 2 of your logbook

03/06

The STEM Cycle of Inquiry: Plan Stage

Once you have asked a question and made an educated guess, you need to plan your investigation. This means deciding what you are going to do to test your guess.

For this investigation:

  • You will use microscopes to observe and record what microorganisms live in the water

04/06

Safety Considerations

When using equipment, we need to always be safe

Read and follow the below safety instructions when using this kit

Electrical Components

Avoid getting water/liquid on the microscope.

Notify an adult of any spills.

Handling Glass

You will be preparing and using glass slides. Ensure careful handling of glass.

Notify an adult if you drop and/or break any glass.

Health and Safety

Do not eat or drink while working with the samples.

Wash your hands after handling samples.

It’s time to do your investigation!

When conducting your investigation, you must keep track of your findings/results.

Draw and label each organism you see in the spaces provided in your logbook (page 4). Be sure to keep track of how many times you see the same organism.

Click the ‘Example’ button to see a worked example for how to fill in your data.

Parts of the Compound Microscope

Key Features:

  • Maximum magnification: 1000X
  • Uses 2 sets of lenses to see samples in 2D
  • Allows you to see through/inside thin samples

02/10

Using a Compound Microscope

Step 1: Adjust your objective lens to the lowest magnification (4X). Be sure to do this using the black dial only

Note: Touching the lenses can damage them.

03/10

Using a Compound Microscope

Step 2: Insert your slide using the stage clip to hold it in place.

04/10

Using a Compound Microscope

Step 3: Turn on the light at the base and use the
illumination dial to adjust the lighting (if needed).

05/10

Using a Compound Microscope

Step 4: Use your X and Y stage controls
to place your sample over the light source.

06/10

Using a Compound Microscope

Step 5: Bring the stage up to the maximum height
using the course focus knob.

Note: Ensure you are at the lowest magnification (4x)
before you do this to avoid hitting the slide into the lens.

07/10

Using a Compound Microscope

Step 6: Looking at the screen or down the eye pieces, use the course focus knob to bring the stage down slightly until you see a clear image.

08/10

Using a Compound Microscope

Step 7: Increase the magnification by switching to the next highest objective lens (yellow 10X). Be sure to do this using the black dial only.

Note: If you get lost at any point after this step, go back to the lowest magnification (4X) and start again.

09/10

Using a Compound Microscope

Step 8: Adjust the fine focus knob to make your image clearer.

Note: You will only need to adjust the fine focus knob from here on, do not adjust the course focus knob or you will bring the sample out of focus.

10/10

Using a Compound Microscope

Step 9: Use the X and Y stage controls to investigate your sample further
